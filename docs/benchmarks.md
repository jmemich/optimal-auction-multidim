# Benchmarks Manifest

The six canonical simulation settings from Chapter 3 of James Michelson's PhD thesis ([sections/auctions.tex](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex)) and the working paper [Kushnir & Michelson 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2207.01664). These settings are the **regression contract** for the `optimal-auction-multidim` refactor — the rewritten code must reproduce these revenues (within numerical tolerance) for each `(setting, T)` cell.

All values originate in the thesis Tables, generated by `BelloniAuctionApproximation` runs on the `fix-distributions` branch of `auctions-qualitative`. Pickled outputs live in [auctions-qualitative/scripts/runs/](../../auctions-qualitative/scripts/runs/) and are loaded by [Full analysis.ipynb](../../phd/ch_auctions_simulations/ebm_code/Full%20analysis.ipynb) (cell 11) to produce the published figures.

Distinct from the approximation algorithm, every setting also has an **exclusive-buyer mechanism (EBM)** revenue at $T=50$, reported in the same Tables. The EBM is the conjectured-optimal mechanism in most of these settings, so EBM revenue is both a regression target and an independent oracle. **The EBM figures are produced by the `ebm_revenue` second-price (Vickrey) simulation in [Full analysis.ipynb](../../phd/ch_auctions_simulations/ebm_code/Full%20analysis.ipynb) (cell 9) — winner pays `p[winner_grade] + max(loser surplus, 0)`, integrated over both buyers — NOT by `ebm.py`'s posted-price `obj`.** The oracle is ported to [`src/optimal_auctions/validation/ebm.py`](../src/optimal_auctions/validation/ebm.py) and pinned in `tests/validation/test_ebm.py`; it reproduces settings 1a–4 exactly.

## Summary

Common across all settings: $N=2$ bidders, $K=2$ quality grades, approximation algorithm with discretization $T \in \{5, 10, 15, 20\}$, EBM at $T=50$.

| # | Name | $V$ | costs | distribution | EBM gap |
|---|------|-----|-------|--------------|---------|
| 1a | Pavlov U[0,1]² | $[0,1] \times [0,1]$ | $[0,0]$ | $X_1, X_2 \sim U[0,1]$ indep. | EBM is conjectured optimal |
| 1b | Pavlov U[2,3]² | $[2,3] \times [2,3]$ | $[0,0]$ | $X_1, X_2 \sim U[2,3]$ indep. | ~1% — stochastic optimal beats EBM |
| 2 | Symmetric Beta(1,2)² | $[0,1] \times [0,1]$ | $[0,0]$ | $X_1, X_2 \sim \text{Beta}(1,2)$ indep. | ~4% |
| 3 | Symmetric correlated $x_1 + x_2$ | $[0,1] \times [0,1]$ | $[0,0]$ | $f(x_1, x_2) = x_1 + x_2$ on unit square | ~3% |
| 4 | Asymmetric Belloni | $[6,8] \times [9,11]$ | $[0.9, 5]$ | $X_1 \sim U[6,8]$, $X_2 \sim U[9,11]$ indep. | (consistency with Belloni et al.) |
| 5 | Asymmetric truncnorm | $[2,3] \times [2,3]$ | $[0, 0]$ ⚠ | $X_1 \sim \mathrm{truncnorm}(\mu{=}2.3, \sigma{=}1, [2,3])$, $X_2 \sim \mathrm{truncnorm}(\mu{=}2.8, \sigma{=}0.2, [2,3])$ | ~5.6% |

⚠ Setting 5 costs are not explicitly stated in the thesis text scanned by Agent 1 — see [Open ambiguities](#open-ambiguities).

⚠¹ **Confirmed thesis typo**: thesis Table [auctions.tex:526-530](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L526) reports 2.58287 for both T=10 and T=15. The T=15 pickle's actual revenue is **2.569334**, which fits monotonically between T=10 (2.582870) and T=20 (2.562314). Thesis duplicated the T=10 cell. *Note*: this run took **400 iterations** (vs 9–195 for the other T values in this setting) — possibly hit an iteration cap or had unusual numerical difficulty; worth verifying convergence before treating as a clean baseline.

⚠² **Small drift, ~0.06%**: pickle gives **5.896792**, thesis Table [auctions.tex:734-738](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L734) reports 5.893113. Difference (3.68e-3) is well above display-rounding. Plausible causes: pickle regenerated after thesis numbers were locked; LP basis-selection non-determinism; or a re-run with slightly different solver settings. Pickle is the regression target; thesis number is kept for trace.

⚠³ **Unnormalised EBM value**: 2.57921 is the *unnormalised* `ebm_revenue` figure for the truncnorm setting — the only setting whose density does not integrate to 1 on the $T=50$ grid (trapezoidal mass $W=0.97717$). The author flagged this call "isn't normalized... don't use". The **correct, normalised** EBM revenue at the thesis reserve $p=[2.56, 2.5]$ is **2.7011591** ($= 2.57921 / W^2$), which is what `tests/validation/test_ebm.py` pins. Settings 1a–4 have $W=1$ (uniform / piecewise-linear densities) so normalised and unnormalised coincide and the published values are exact.

## Per-setting detail

### 1a — Pavlov U[0,1]² (symmetric, independent, uniform)

The Pavlov 2011 benchmark. $N=1$ has a closed-form optimum (deterministic, reserve $1/\sqrt 3$); for $N \ge 2$ the approximation algorithm is needed.

- **Config**: $N=2$, $K=2$, $V = [[0,1], [0,1]]$, $\text{costs} = [0, 0]$, $X_1, X_2 \sim U[0,1]$ i.i.d.
- **Thesis**: setup at [auctions.tex:388-389](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L388); revenue Table at [auctions.tex:452-461](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L452); fig:pavlov_n2_alloc at [auctions.tex:468-476](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L468).
- **Pickles**: `symmetric_independent_unif_01_{5,10,15,20}.pkl` in [auctions-qualitative/scripts/runs/](../../auctions-qualitative/scripts/runs/).
- **Generating notebook**: [Full analysis.ipynb](../../phd/ch_auctions_simulations/ebm_code/Full%20analysis.ipynb), Setting 1a section (cells 12–25 approx).
- **Theoretical baselines**: $N=1$ reserve $p^* = 1/\sqrt{3}$; $N=1$ revenue $p^*(1-p^{*2}) = 2 / (3\sqrt 3) \approx 0.3849$.

### 1b — Pavlov U[2,3]² (symmetric, independent, uniform; *stochastic optimal*)

Same family as 1a but shifted support. This is the case where the optimal mechanism **requires randomization** — EBM is no longer optimal. The thesis uses this to demonstrate that the approximation algorithm can detect stochasticity.

- **Config**: $N=2$, $K=2$, $V = [[2,3], [2,3]]$, $\text{costs} = [0, 0]$, $X_1, X_2 \sim U[2,3]$ i.i.d.
- **Thesis**: setup at [auctions.tex:388](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L388); revenue Table at [auctions.tex:526-541](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L526); fig:pavlov_n2_23_alloc at [auctions.tex:542-550](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L542).
- **Pickles**: `symmetric_independent_unif_23_{5,10,15,20}.pkl`.
- **Generating notebook**: [Full analysis.ipynb](../../phd/ch_auctions_simulations/ebm_code/Full%20analysis.ipynb), Setting 1b section (cells 25–36 approx).
- **Notes**: The reported $T=10$ and $T=15$ revenues are byte-identical (`2.58287`). Verify whether this is a true plateau in the algorithm output or a copy-paste artifact in the thesis Table. Per math-vigilance, do not silently match this in baselines.json.

### 2 — Symmetric Beta(1,2)² (symmetric, independent, non-uniform)

Non-uniform symmetric setting. EBM gap is the second-largest (~4%) among settings where EBM is conjectured optimal.

- **Config**: $N=2$, $K=2$, $V = [[0,1], [0,1]]$, $\text{costs} = [0, 0]$, $X_1, X_2 \sim \text{Beta}(\alpha{=}1, \beta{=}2)$ i.i.d. (PDF $f(x) = 2(1-x)$ on $[0,1]$).
- **Thesis**: setup at [auctions.tex:390](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L390) (cites Daskalakis et al. 2017); revenue Table at [auctions.tex:572-581](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L572); fig:beta12_alloc at [auctions.tex:586-594](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L586).
- **Pickles**: `symmetric_independent_beta_{5,10,15,20}.pkl`.
- **Generating notebook**: [Full analysis.ipynb](../../phd/ch_auctions_simulations/ebm_code/Full%20analysis.ipynb), Setting 2 section (cells 37–48 approx).
- **Notes**: Reserve $p^* = 0.4$ holds across $N \in \{1, 2, 3\}$ — load-bearing for the conjecture that exclusion-region geometry is $N$-invariant. Verify in baselines.

### 3 — Symmetric correlated $x_1 + x_2$ (symmetric, correlated)

Correlated symmetric setting with a custom joint density. **Not equivalent** to `examples.py`'s `ex4` (which uses a convex mixture $\alpha f_1 + (1-\alpha) f_2$, not the additive joint).

- **Config**: $N=2$, $K=2$, $V = [[0,1], [0,1]]$, $\text{costs} = [0, 0]$, joint density $f(x_1, x_2) \propto x_1 + x_2$ on $[0,1]^2$. The proportionality constant normalises the density: $\int_0^1\!\int_0^1 (x_1+x_2)\,dx_1\,dx_2 = 1$, so the PDF is exactly $x_1 + x_2$ (no rescaling needed).
- **Thesis**: setup at [auctions.tex:392-393](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L392); revenue Table at [auctions.tex:653-662](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L653); fig:symmetric_correlated_alloc at [auctions.tex:667-675](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L667).
- **Pickles**: `symmetric_correlated_unif_{5,10,15,20}.pkl`.
- **Generating notebook**: [Full analysis.ipynb](../../phd/ch_auctions_simulations/ebm_code/Full%20analysis.ipynb), Setting 3 section (cells 49–59 approx).
- **Notes**: Reserve $p^* = 0.65$ across $N \in \{1, 2, 3\}$. Don't reuse `examples.py:ex4` as a regression source — it's a different distribution.

### 4 — Belloni asymmetric uniform with costs

The asymmetric setting from Belloni et al. 2010, used so this code's outputs can be cross-checked against published values from that paper's Table 3.

- **Config**: $N=2$, $K=2$, $V = [[6,8], [9,11]]$, $\text{costs} = [0.9, 5]$, $X_1 \sim U[6,8]$, $X_2 \sim U[9,11]$ indep.
- **Thesis**: setup at [auctions.tex:394-395](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L394); revenue Table at [auctions.tex:734-743](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L734); fig:belloni_alloc at [auctions.tex:748-756](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L748); fig:belloni_alloc_alln at [auctions.tex:762-770](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L762); fig:belloni_alloc_Q1 at [auctions.tex:883-891](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L883) discusses possible randomization in the asymmetric case.
- **Pickles**: `asymmetric_independent_belloni_{5,10,15,20}.pkl`.
- **Generating notebook**: [Full analysis.ipynb](../../phd/ch_auctions_simulations/ebm_code/Full%20analysis.ipynb), Setting 4 section ([cell 60+](../../phd/ch_auctions_simulations/ebm_code/Full%20analysis.ipynb)). Cell 70 uses EBM reserves $p = [6, 10.2]$.
- **External cross-check**: Belloni, Lopomo, Wang 2010, Table 3.
- **Notes**: EBM-comparison is for consistency, not optimality — the thesis suggests this setting *may* require randomization (see fig:belloni_alloc_Q1 discussion at [auctions.tex:883](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L883)). Verify the pickle's stored config matches the thesis (especially $V$ and $\text{costs}$): one of the read-only research agents earlier reported a discrepancy that was likely an agent misread, but it's worth re-checking on unpickle.

### 5 — Asymmetric truncnorm

The largest EBM gap (~5.6%) — a setting where neither the approximation algorithm nor EBM is fully converged at the $T$ values tested.

- **Config**: $N=2$, $K=2$, $V = [[2,3], [2,3]]$, $\text{costs} = [0, 0]$, $X_1 \sim \mathrm{truncnorm}(\mu{=}2.3, \sigma{=}1)$ truncated to $[2, 3]$, $X_2 \sim \mathrm{truncnorm}(\mu{=}2.8, \sigma{=}0.2)$ truncated to $[2, 3]$. Exact `scipy.stats` reconstruction:
  ```python
  from scipy.stats import truncnorm
  X1 = truncnorm(a=(2.0 - 2.3) / 1.0,  b=(3.0 - 2.3) / 1.0,  loc=2.3, scale=1.0)
  X2 = truncnorm(a=(2.0 - 2.8) / 0.2, b=(3.0 - 2.8) / 0.2, loc=2.8, scale=0.2)
  ```
  Pickles store the standardised `a, b` directly: $X_1$ uses `(a≈-0.3, b≈0.7, loc=2.3, scale=1.0)`, $X_2$ uses `(a≈-4.0, b≈1.0, loc=2.8, scale=0.2)`. Verified across all four T values.
- **Thesis**: setup at [auctions.tex:396](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L396); revenue Table at [auctions.tex:792-801](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L792); fig:truncnorm_alloc at [auctions.tex:806-814](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L806); reserve discussion at [auctions.tex:818](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L818).
- **Pickles**: `asymmetric_independent_truncnorm_{5,10,15,20}.pkl`.
- **Generating notebook**: [Full analysis.ipynb](../../phd/ch_auctions_simulations/ebm_code/Full%20analysis.ipynb), Setting 5 section (cell 74+).
- **Notes**: Reserves $p_1^* = 2.55$, $p_2^* = 2.50$ constant across $N \in \{1, 2, 3\}$. Truncnorm parametrisation in `scipy.stats.truncnorm` requires standardising bounds (`a = (low - μ)/σ`, `b = (high - μ)/σ`) — easy to get wrong; cross-check the truncated-PDF integrates to 1 on $[2,3]$ before treating outputs as canonical.

## Other thesis baselines worth noting

Not full settings, but referenced in the math and useful as sanity checks:

- **Pavlov $N=1$ closed form**: deterministic optimum, reserve $p^* = 1/\sqrt{3} \approx 0.5774$, revenue $\approx 0.3849$ ([auctions.tex:404](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L404)). Good unit test for the $K=2, N=1$ degenerate case.
- **Pavlov upper-bound on optimality gain** ($N=1$, $X \sim U[c, c+1]^2$): randomisation gains at most ~1.2% over deterministic ([auctions.tex:865](../../phd/sections/auctions.tex#L865), Pavlov 2011 p.11). Useful as a theoretical ceiling on the "stochastic optimal vs EBM" gap in setting 1b.
- **Myerson $K=1, N=1$**: closed-form Myerson reserve for any 1-D distribution. Trivial-but-canonical regression target for the degenerate-dimension case (sanity check that the LP reduces correctly when $K=1$).

## How to use this doc

- **As a regression target**: each row of the revenue table is a `(setting, T) → expected_value` test case. `baselines.json` will codify these.
- **As a config dictionary**: per-setting configs are the inputs to construct an `OptimalAuctionApproximation` (refactored name TBD: `OptimalAuctionApproximation` per current public API).
- **As a known-bug ledger**: the [Open ambiguities](#open-ambiguities) list is the running list of things to check when extracting baselines. Update this section as items are resolved.
